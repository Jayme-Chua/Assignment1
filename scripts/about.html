The artist

Christian Friedrich Johannes Büttner (born 1 June 1979), known professionally as TheFatRat, is a Youtube music artist, DJ and record producer. He was born in Göttingen, Germany.

Music career

Buttner began producing music under his own name in 2001. He began by producing background music for television, radio, and advertising, and then produced music for DJs.
In 2010, Buttner produced the song "Mignon Mignon" for René La Torpe, which became a number one single in France. Around the same time, he also produced the song "Audubon Ballroom" for Lupe Fiasco's album "Food & Liquor II: The Great American Rap Album Pt. 1," which reached #5 on the Billboard 200.
In July 2011, he began producing solo music under the pseudonym TheFatRat, a nickname given to him in his youth. Büttner's career began with the release of his first EP, Do Be Do Be Do.
By 2016, Buttner's music had been used in over 1.5 million YouTube videos. His single "Unity" (from the album Sounds of Syndication Vol. 1) was curated by Tom Cassell, one of the most-watched streamers on Twitch.
In November 2016, Buttner released his second EP, "Jackpot", on the Universal Music label; the EP reached #23 on Billboard's Dance/Electronic Albums chart; in December 2016, Buttner joined forces with Universal Music record label, The Arcadium.
In June 2017, he released the single "Fly Away" featuring vocalist Angelee; in July, he performed at the Electric Forest Festival. In October 2017, he released the single "Oblivion" featuring vocalist Lola Blanc. "Oblivion" was released.
In March 2018, he released the single "MAYDAY" In July 2018, he released "Warrior Songs", a music pack for the video game Dota 2, available in the game on September 13, 2018, according to Team Secret's Operations Director Matthew Bailey (aka Cyborg Matt) stated.
In December 2018, he posted a video about how YouTube's content ID system is "broken," which he said is "a very good thing."
This was because one anonymous user known as Ramjets claimed all the revenue for his song "The Calling" and YouTube refused to settle the dispute. This claim was later removed by YouTube.
In February 2020, Redbull.com created a 10-minute documentary about Butner and his career, "TheFatRat: Everything You Ever Wanted To Know About Him." They released this in the article "TheFatRat: Meet the EDM DJ who's elevated video game music to high art.
